# Communication

Documents that keep people aligned on what's happening — status, decisions, reflections, and plans. These are the connective tissue of a company. Without them, teams drift apart and context lives only in the heads of whoever was in the room. Good communication docs make the implicit explicit so everyone moves in the same direction.

## Audience

Varies by document. Status updates go to leadership and stakeholders. Retrospectives are for the team that did the work. Kickoff docs are for everyone involved in an upcoming effort. The common thread: these are written for people who need to stay informed without being in every conversation.

## Document Types

| Document | Purpose | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Project Kickoff | The opening document for a new initiative — what we're doing, why now, who's involved, and what success looks like. Aligns the team before work begins | — |
| Status Update / Weekly Summary | What happened this week, what's blocked, and what's coming next — the recurring pulse check that keeps stakeholders informed without requiring meetings | — |
| Retrospective | What went well, what didn't, and what we're changing — the structured reflection after a project or incident that turns experience into improvement | — |
